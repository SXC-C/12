Tourism in Catalonia

Home Catalonia Canary Islands Andalusia

Coves of deep blue sea, beaches of golden sand, natural parks, medieval towns, the works of Salvador Dalí… This is the tempting combination of Girona's Costa Brava.

This is the area of Catalonia from Blanes to Portbou with fascinating cities like Girona and old fishing villages with little whitewashed houses like Cadaqués. Undoubtedly a great place to enjoy the pleasant climate of the Mediterranean, visit some of its Michelin-starred restaurants, or follow the Empordà D.O. Wine Route. The Costa Brava is always a good choice for a holiday destination.

Six fast facts

Population: 300,000 to 350,000
Area about 200 kilometers (124 miles)
The Costa Brava is located along the northeastern coast of Spain
The Medes Islands just off the coast of Costa Brava,
are a protected marine reserve and one of the top diving spots in the Mediterranean, known for rich marine life.
Costa Brava is renowned for its pristine beaches,
many of which have been awarded Blue Flag status for cleanliness and environmental standards.
Greek and Roman Ruins: The ancient ruins of Empúries, a former Greek and Roman settlement,
are located in Costa Brava, offering a fascinating glimpse into early Mediterranean civilizations.
